8. Tips and General Advice

I'll now give a few tips to making a good Monocurl slideshow or video.

8.1 Tips

  1. Remember everything is short lived. Don't over engineer stuff and be ok with using hardcoded constants. Most of the time, you'll never touch it again. Still maintain some level of professionalism, but only so much that is necessary
  2. Use resources effectively! Look at examples. I also tried to make the documentation highlight which meshes are very important, and which ones you probably only have to lookup if necessary.
  3. Follow the iterator-follower paradigm. I know it's verbose, but that's the best way to do Monocurl
  4. Maintain sane tags, and partition variables appropriately
  5. Create lots of functions for meshes, and few for animations. There are times where you might need to create functions for animations (we'll talk about a case, specifically when we have to apply a transformation with a discrete transformation together), but these are the exception. More often than not, using a cleverly calculated functor with an interpolation will seal the deal
  6. Break down complex animations as a parallel and sequential subcompnenents, and think about interpolation
  7. Break down complex animations as subcompoenntets, and operations on primtiives
